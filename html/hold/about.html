Basic Query Logic

If you're a Voyager record, you probably have member copy if you have...

  • an ISBN that matches a WorldCat record, and if that WorldCat record has...
    • A call number in the 050 and/or 090 (including numbers, 0-9)
    • Either a subject (600, 610, 611, 65x other than 653) OR literary form (008/33) other than "0" or " "

Common checks

For all reports...

From Voyager data
  • MFHD call no.: no
  • Item Status: In Process
  • 902: no
  • Locations (non-SA): anxb, dixn, f, mus, sd, se, sh, st, sv, zeis, srel, ssrc, sxf, vidl, piapr, anxa, dc, dr, ppl, sc, scp, shs, slav, spc, spia, spr, anxbl, rcppa, clas, docs, fis, rcppk, rcpqk, scl, sci, egsr
  • Locations (SA): sa, saph, rcppj
  • Either a subject (600, 610, 611, 65x other than 653) *or* literary form (008/33) is something other than "0" or " "
From WorldCat data
  • leader/18 - encoding level
  • 008/23 - NOT 'o' or 's' (exclude e-records)
  • 008/35-37 - language (OCLC lang, not Voyager lang, is included in final reports)
  • 008/33 - literary form
  • 001 - OCLC number
  • 042$a - authentication code (pcc column)
  • 050_ _ - LC call number (lc column)
  • 090 - shelf location
  • 600, 610, 611, 65X other than 653 - subjects

Note

By default, a given record is checked against WorldCat every 30 days. This is for the sake of efficiency and good relations with our friends at OCLC.
